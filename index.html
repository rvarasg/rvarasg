MY THESIS

My PhD thesis is not very prototipical. I come from an engineering background, but I have gone through astronomy too. Because of this, my work shares a bit of both worlds. I am part of the CARMENES consortium, and the first part of my thesis consisted in upgrading the CARMENES-NIR channel performance, with excellent results (Varas et al. 2025). Then, I started working with CARMENES data, in particular with the coolest stars among the CARMENES sample, the ultracool dwarfs. I try to better understand their behaviour and look for exoplanets around them.

SPECULOOS-3

One of the more exciting projects I have participated on is the descovery of an earh-like planet around the SPECULOOS-3 star. This is a big colaboration around the globe, and together with my colleagues at the IAA and CARMENES data, we were able to help on this study. Our main contribution was to estimate the projected rotational velocity of the star using our CARMENES data. Nevertheless, we have not stopped working on this system, because it is fascinating. We still have more radial velocity data, and we are carefully analysing it.

CARMENES-PLUS

A big part of my early work in the PhD thesis involved engineering task. CARMENES near-infrared channel (or CARMENES-NIR) is cooled down to achieve very precise radial velcity measurements. Our main targets are very faint stars, the M-dwarfs. Because they are very faint, the thermal conditions we need in CARMENES-NIR are very extreme. We worked from 2020 to 2022 improving its cooling system. By stabilizing the nitrogen gas flow and refilling properly the liquid nitrogen, we were able to improve the CARMENES-NIR radial velocity precision below the 1 m/s.

Projected rotational velocity and limb darkening
×

PROJECTED ROTATIONAL VELOCITY

The projected rotational velocity (v·sin(i)) of a star is a very important value, because we can then properly derive other stellar parameters, better undersanding the star, such as its age, metallicity or activity. For fast rotating stars is also key for accuartely compute the radial velocities for exoplanet search. I have computed the projected rotational velocity of all the CARMENES sample using the SERVAL pipeline. I made a extensive analysis of the limb darkening effect when computing this parameter, specially important for the coolest stars.
