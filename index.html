My research

I am a last-year PhD researcher at the IAA (Granada, Spain). I work both in the instrument and the science exploitation, mainly with CARMENES. I use the near-infrared channel (NIR) to characterize M-dwarfs stars and hunt exoplanets orbiting them. I am specially interested in the coolest stars, even beyond the sub-stellar regime (brown dwarfs). Like every astronomer, I collaborate with people around the world.
If you want to know more about my studies and career, please check my cv.

My outreach

One part that is not strictly part of my job but that I really enjoying doing, is outreach. Sharing my passion about astronomy, that I have the privilage of being my work too, is a very satisfing feeling. After a talk or activity with a very general public, from children to adults and with many different backgrounds, there always appear awesome questions and disscusions. being able to talk with so many people and enjoy an activity together of a common passion or interest is very enriching.

DEI

Currently, I am a member of the Diversity, equity and inclusion cometee at the IAA. We work to make our institute a safer and more inclusive environment for everybod. We also do outreach and visibility activities. I believe that a more inclusive and diverse science is better. Having variety of experiences, feelings and ways of thinking is how great science is done. If you are interested, I really encourage you to go and check out our activities and work.
